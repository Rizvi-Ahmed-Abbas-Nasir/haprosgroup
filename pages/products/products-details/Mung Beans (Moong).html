Culinary Uses

Moong is a wholesome, nutrient-dense flour made from a blend of different millets, such as bajra (pearl millet), ragi (finger millet), jowar (sorghum), and others. It’s rich in fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals, making it a healthy alternative to refined flours. Some common uses of multi-millet flour include: Making flatbreads like roti, chapati, and paratha Preparing dosas and uttapams Using in porridge and breakfast cereals Baking healthy snacks like millet cookies and pancakes Adding to soups and stews for a nutty flavor and extra nutrition
